Mosaic Draconia: The Princess' Gambit

GAMEPLAY OVERVIEW

  1. Objective of the Game:
    • To become the sovereign ruler of Draconia by earning the most Victory Points (VPs) through strategic conquests, clever alliances, and collection of mystical artifacts.
  2. Setup:
    • Lay out the game board, which is divided into territories forming a mosaic of different landscapes.
    • Each player chooses a faction represented by unique 6-layered chess pawns.
    • Shuffle the deck of Dragon, Princess, and Mosaic cards. Place them facedown by the board.
    • Each player rolls the nine-sided dice to determine the play order.
  3. Game Components:
    • Game Board: A hexagonal grid with various terrain types.
    • Pawns: Each pawn has six layers, each layer representing different abilities.
    • Dice: Custom nine-sided dice corresponding to the nine realms of Draconia.
    • Cards: Three types of cards – Dragon, Princess, and Mosaic cards.
  4. Gameplay:
    • Players take turns in a clockwise order.
    • Beginning of Turn:
      • Draw one Mosaic card.
      • Roll the nine-sided dice to determine available resources and actions.
    • Actions:
      • Conquest: Use resources to conquer adjacent territories.
      • Trade: Negotiate and trade resources with other players.
      • Build: Erect buildings that grant VPs and special abilities.
      • Summon: Play Dragon or Princess cards to summon mythical creatures or royal allies to aid your conquest.
    • End of Turn:
      • Check for VPs from newly conquered territories, buildings, or completed mosaics.
  5. Pawns and Layers:
    • Each layer of a pawn grants different abilities; players may activate one layer per turn.
    • Layers can be locked or unlocked by using specific Mosaic cards.
    • Layers impact combat, resource generation, and special abilities.
  6. Dragon and Princess Cards:
    • Dragon Cards: Summon dragons for combat or to guard territories.
    • Princess Cards: Summon princesses for diplomatic advantages or to invoke special rules.
  7. Mosaic Cards:
    • Grant resources, special actions, or immediate VPs.
    • Some Mosaic cards are Wildcards, allowing players to mimic other player’s actions or break standard rules.
  8. Combat:
    • Resolved by dice rolls modified by pawn layers and any summoned dragons.
    • Defeated pawns are returned to the player’s reserve.
  9. Special Abilities and Wildcards:
    • Certain cards and pawn layers grant abilities that can alter the state of play, such as double resource collection or extra turns.
    • Wildcards can be played at any time and can drastically change the outcome of a player's turn.
  10. Victory Points (VPs):
    • Earned through conquering territories, building structures, completing mosaics, and collecting sets of Dragon or Princess cards.
    • Some territories and mosaics grant more VPs than others.
  11. End Game:
    • The game ends when a player reaches a predetermined number of VPs or all territories are claimed.
    • The player with the most VPs at the end of the game is declared the ruler of Draconia.
  12. Exceptions:
    • If a player rolls a 9 on the dice, they invoke the “Curse of the Ancients,” which allows them to steal a card from another player.
    • If a player has no moves available, they may discard a Mosaic card to re-roll the dice.
  13. Tie-Breakers:
    • In case of a tie, the player with the most complete sets of Dragon and Princess cards wins.
    • If still tied, the player with the most layers unlocked on their pawns wins.
